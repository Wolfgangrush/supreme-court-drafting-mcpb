## Section 1 — Statutory Opening (Art 32)

```
WRIT PETITION (CIVIL / CRIMINAL) UNDER ARTICLE 32 OF THE CONSTITUTION OF INDIA SEEKING [Mandamus / Certiorari / Habeas Corpus / Prohibition / Quo Warranto / Declaratory Writ in the nature of...] FOR ENFORCEMENT OF FUNDAMENTAL RIGHTS GUARANTEED UNDER ARTICLE(S) [14 / 19 / 20 / 21 / etc.] OF THE CONSTITUTION OF INDIA.
```

## Section 2 — Cause Title (Art 32)

```
IN THE SUPREME COURT OF INDIA
CIVIL ORIGINAL JURISDICTION (or CRIMINAL ORIGINAL JURISDICTION for Habeas Corpus / quashing)

Writ Petition (Civil / Criminal) No. _____ of [Year]

(Petition under Article 32 of the Constitution of India seeking enforcement of
fundamental rights under Article(s) [____])

  IN THE MATTER OF:

  [Petitioner Name]                                ... Petitioner

                          VERSUS

  Union of India / State of [State] / [Respondent Name]      ... Respondent(s)
```

## Section 3 — "No alternative efficacious remedy" paragraph

```
The Petitioner has no other equally efficacious remedy available to enforce the fundamental right(s) invoked except by way of the present petition under Article 32 of the Constitution of India. [Where Art 226 was previously invoked or could have been — add a paragraph explaining the choice of Art 32 over Art 226.]
```

## Section 4 — PIL credentials paragraph (per *Balwant Singh Chaufal*)

```
[Where the Petition is filed as a PIL — i.e., the Petitioner is not personally aggrieved:]

The Petitioner is [profession / civic role / public-interest credential]. The information forming the basis of this Petition has been gathered from [source — RTI / press reports / personal observation / etc.]. The Petitioner has no personal interest in the litigation save the public interest sought to be advanced.
```

## Section 5 — Main Prayer (Art 32 writ-form)

```
In the premises aforesaid, it is most respectfully prayed that this Hon'ble Court may graciously be pleased to:

(a) Issue a writ of [Mandamus / Certiorari / Habeas Corpus / etc.] directing Respondent No. 1 to [specific direction];

(b) Issue any other or further writ, order or direction as this Hon'ble Court may deem fit and proper in the facts and circumstances of the case and in the interest of justice.
```
