## Section 3 — Grounds (Rupa Hurra three-limb framing)

```
GROUNDS

A. VIOLATION OF PRINCIPLES OF NATURAL JUSTICE

BECAUSE the Petitioner was not heard / was inadequately heard / was not given a fair opportunity to be heard before the impugned judgment was passed, in that [specific procedural defect — failure to hear on a material issue / failure to notice a controlling submission / etc.].

B. APPREHENSION OF BIAS

BECAUSE there existed circumstances raising a reasonable apprehension of bias on the part of the Hon'ble Judge(s) who passed the impugned judgment, which circumstances were not within the knowledge of the Petitioner at the time of the original proceeding / Review Petition. The specific circumstance is [specific fact establishing the apprehension — financial connection / relationship / prior expressed view / etc.] which has come to the Petitioner's knowledge only after the dismissal of the Review Petition.

C. ANY OTHER SUBSTANTIAL CURATIVE GROUND (narrow per Yakub Memon)

BECAUSE absent curative intervention, the judgment will perpetuate a manifest miscarriage of justice in that [specific ground — read narrowly per the law laid down in *Yakub Memon v. State of Maharashtra* (2015) 9 SCC 552].
```

(a) Reopen the judgment / order dated <DD.MM.YYYY> passed by this Hon'ble Court in [Original Case Type & Number] and pass such orders as are necessary to prevent the miscarriage of justice that would otherwise result;

(b) Pending hearing and final disposal of the present Curative Petition, stay the operation of the said judgment / order;
