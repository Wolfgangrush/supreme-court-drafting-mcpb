## Section 1 — Statutory Opening (Review Petition)

```
REVIEW PETITION UNDER ARTICLE 137 OF THE CONSTITUTION OF INDIA READ WITH ORDER XLVII OF THE SUPREME COURT RULES, 2013 AND ORDER XLVII RULE 1 OF THE CODE OF CIVIL PROCEDURE, 1908 FOR REVIEW OF THE JUDGMENT / ORDER DATED <DD.MM.YYYY> PASSED BY THIS HON'BLE COURT IN [Case Type & Number].
```

## Section 2 — Grounds for Review (three-limb framing)

```
GROUNDS

A. ORDER XLVII RULE 1(a) — DISCOVERY OF NEW AND IMPORTANT MATTER

BECAUSE after the impugned judgment was delivered, the Petitioner has come to know of [new evidence / new matter] which, despite the exercise of due diligence, was not within the Petitioner's knowledge and could not have been produced before this Hon'ble Court. [Specific particulars of the new matter, with reference to the supporting annexure.]

B. ORDER XLVII RULE 1(b) — MISTAKE OR ERROR APPARENT ON THE FACE OF THE RECORD

BECAUSE the impugned judgment proceeds on a mistake / error apparent on the face of the record in that [specific paragraph of the original judgment where the error is visible]. The error is patent and does not require elaborate argument to demonstrate.

C. ORDER XLVII RULE 1(c) — ANY OTHER SUFFICIENT REASON

BECAUSE [specific ground falling within the narrow scope of "any other sufficient reason" as construed in the line of authority including *Northern India Caterers* and *Lily Thomas*].
```

## Section 3 — Main Prayer

```
In the premises aforesaid, it is most respectfully prayed that this Hon'ble Court may graciously be pleased to:

(a) Review and set aside / modify / recall the judgment and order dated <DD.MM.YYYY> passed by this Hon'ble Court in [Case Type & Number];

(b) Pending hearing and final disposal of the present Review Petition, stay the operation of the said impugned judgment and order;

(c) Pass any other or further order(s) as this Hon'ble Court may deem fit and proper.
```
